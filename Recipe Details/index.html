Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian dish that originated in Rome. Itâ€™s a rich and creamy pasta dish made with spaghetti, eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and guanciale (Italian bacon). The dish is often misunderstood and misinterpreted, but when done correctly, itâ€™s a culinary delight. The authentic recipe involves cooking spaghetti al dente, then mixing it with a sauce made from eggs, cheese, and guanciale. The eggs are cooked with the pasta, creating a creamy and rich sauce. The dish is often served with a sprinkle of black pepper and extra Pecorino Romano cheese.

Time: 20 minutes

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 slices of guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
Cook the guanciale or pancetta. In a large skillet, cook the guanciale or pancetta over medium heat until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
Whisk the eggs and Parmesan cheese. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
Combine the cooked spaghetti and guanciale or pancetta. Add the cooked spaghetti to the bowl with the guanciale or pancetta and toss to combine.
Add the egg mixture and pasta water. Pour the egg mixture over the spaghetti and guanciale or pancetta mixture. Toss everything together, adding some reserved pasta water if needed to achieve a creamy sauce.
Season with salt and black pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
Serve immediately. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.
